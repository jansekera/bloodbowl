## TL;DR — the brief's prime hypothesis is REFUTED, and the cause is deeper than wiring

1. **The value net is NOT collapsed — it discriminates.** Directly measured: global
   output std ≈ 0.33–0.58, value range across legal successors ≈ 0.57–0.78 on
   [-1,1], no saturation. So "value can't tell positions apart → flat target" is
   **wrong**.
2. **The policy head DID learn.** On the trained weights it beats uniform on the
   real target: KL(target‖policy)=0.096 vs KL(target‖uniform)=0.133 (improvement
   +0.037); top3_recall 0.57. The "flat loss ~2.24 / top1 ~38%" is a metric
   artifact — cross-entropy is lower-bounded by the target entropy (H_norm≈0.94),
   and **`top1_agree` is misleading** (high-entropy, multi-modal target, ~36%
   near-ties → top-1 is unhittable by construction).
3. **Real cause located in SEARCH, not the heads or features.** In the runs that
   produced the flat target, both learned heads were wired OUT of MCTS
   (`vfBlend=0` at the leaf — `macro_mcts.cpp:496`; `policy_blend=0` in priors —
   `macro_mcts.cpp:188`). The target was generated by a hand-coded heuristic.
4. **But connecting the heads is NOT sufficient (measured).** A direct sweep of
   `simulate_game_logged` with the heads connected does NOT concentrate visits:

   | vf_blend | policy_blend | H_norm | top1 |
   |---:|---:|---:|---:|
   | 0.0 | 0.0  | 0.943 | 0.268 |
   | 0.5 | 0.0  | 0.937 | 0.264 |
   | 0.0 | 0.15 | 0.944 | 0.260 |
   | 0.5 | 0.15 | 0.937 | 0.272 |
   | 1.0 | 0.30 | 0.928 | 0.268 |

   Even fully on (1.0, 0.30) only reaches H_norm 0.928 — far from the ~0.70 success
   bar. So the binding constraint is the **MCTS visit-allocation mechanism itself**.

5. **Per-player features would not fix this** (consensus of all three members). The
   value already discriminates; the bottleneck is downstream in search.

## Current lead — MCTS quality (open-loop macro Q-variance)
`MacroMCTSSearch` backprops a single noisy rollout with fresh dice each iteration
(`macro_mcts.cpp:509-540`) → high-variance Q estimates → PUCT cannot separate
macros → diffuse visits even with a good value at the leaf. Next experiments
(team1-mcts-quality / task T2): (step 1, no rebuild) sweep simulation budget and
`exploration_c`; (step 2, C++ change) average multiple rollouts / closed-loop
evaluation to cut Q variance, then re-measure H_norm/KL/strength.

## Secondary, separate issue — value-training target (drives gate rejects, not flatness)
`mc_shaped` regresses a one-step shaped reward with the same ±1 outcome broadcast
to every state of a game, no discounted-return accumulation
(`trainer.py:421-483`, `replay_buffer.py:50-69`). This is high-variance credit
assignment and a likely driver of the 89%→85% value drift and gate REJECTs — but
it is NOT the cause of the flat policy target (the value still discriminates).

## Update — T2 step 1 (sims / exploration_c sweep, measured)
With both heads connected (vf=0.5, pol=0.15), `exploration_c` is a real lever; the
training/ceiling default of 2.0 was over-exploring and flattening the target:

| sims | exploration_c | H_norm | top1 |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 100 | 2.0 | 0.936 | 0.283 |
| 800 | 2.0 | 0.926 | 0.271 |
| 400 | 1.0 | 0.923 | 0.267 |
| 400 | 0.5 | 0.855 | 0.337 |
| 800 | 0.5 | 0.812 | 0.400 |

More sims alone barely helps (0.936→0.926); lowering `exploration_c` to 0.5 (with
800 sims) drops H_norm to 0.812 and lifts top1 to 0.40 — meaningful, but still above
the ~0.70 bar. So `exploration_c=0.5` is a cheap config win; proceed to step 2 (cut
open-loop macro Q-variance in `macro_mcts.cpp:509-540`).

## Update — T2 step 2 (averaging K rollouts, measured): NO EFFECT
Added an `n_rollouts` knob (average K open-loop rollouts per leaf eval; default 1 =
unchanged) and measured at `exploration_c=0.5`, heads connected:

| sims | n_rollouts | H_norm | top1 |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 400 | 1 | 0.830 | 0.374 |
| 400 | 4 | 0.825 | 0.345 |
| 400 | 8 | 0.839 | 0.341 |
| 800 | 4 | 0.792 | 0.380 |

Increasing K does nothing (0.830→0.825→0.839, within noise; top1 does not rise). The
small gain at 800/K=4 is from the extra sims, not K. **Open-loop macro Q-variance is
NOT the binding constraint.**

### Where this leaves the search investigation
Levers tried: wiring (no), more sims (small), `exploration_c` (REAL: H_norm
0.94→0.81), rollout averaging (no). The one cheap, real win is **`exploration_c≈0.5`**.
The residual H_norm ~0.81 looks largely irreducible by search — consistent with many
Blood Bowl macro decisions being genuinely near-equivalent (move-forward,
interchangeable linemen), i.e. a partly *correct* flat target. Next: (T3) fix the
value-training target to address value drift / gate rejects, plus a validation
TRAINING run with `exploration_c=0.5` to confirm the sharper target → stronger play.
(The `n_rollouts` knob is retained default-off for reproducibility; revert if unused.)
